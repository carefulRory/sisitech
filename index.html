Barack Obama

Barack Hussein Obama II is an American attorney and politician who served as the 44th President of the United States from 2009 to 2017. He is a member of the Democratic Party and was the first African American president.

Born in Honolulu, Hawaii, Obama is a graduate of Columbia University and Harvard Law School. He is known for his charismatic speeches and his policies on healthcare, climate change, and international relations.

Barack Obama's presidency was marked by significant legislative achievements, including the Affordable Care Act, the Paris Agreement on climate change, and the normalization of relations with Cuba.
